Feilberg, Carl Adolf

 

(Source: Flora Malesiana ser. 1, 5: Cyclopaedia of collectors, Supplement I)

 

Born: 1844, Vester Vedsted ved Ribe, Denmark. Died: 1937.

 

career:

Studied medicine at Odense in 1863; in 1864 he volunteered in the war, was made a prisoner, but escaped; subsequently he studied botany. In 1867 he made a trip to the Farøer; from 1868-69 the Danish Botanical Society gave him the opportunity to travel to East India, China, and Further India. In 1874 M.D. and subsequently as a surgeon on a German ship to New Zealand; since 1876 practising in Copenhagen, and on the Øresund Hospital; retired in 1916.

 

collections:

Herb. Copenhagen [C]: 282 nos from Bombay, Hongkong, Bangkok, Saigon, and Singapore (1), leg. 1868-69.
