Norris, Sir William

(Source: Flora Malesiana ser. 1, 5: Cyclopaedia of collectors, Supplement I)

(Source: Flora Malesiana ser. 1, 8: Cyclopaedia of collectors, Supplement II)

 

Born: 1793, London, England. Died: 1859, Sunningdale, Berkshire, England.

 

career:

A jurist who went to British India in 1829; from 1836-47 in the Malay Peninsula (Recorder of Penang etc.), later Chief Justice in Ceylon. He was a friend of Griffith’s.

He is commemorated in the genus Norrisia Gardn. and in some other plant species.

 

Collecting localities:

1836-47. Malay Peninsula: Penang; Mt Ophir (Johore), Malacca.

 

collections:

He sent plants to Griffith, Sir W.J. Hooker, Lady Dalhousie (see those), and Gardner in Ceylon.1 The herbaria of Griffith, Hooker and Lady Dalhousie came to Kew [K] and so did Norris’s own fern collection. Herb. Berl. [B]: Malacca plants (pres. c. 1849); also dupl. in Herb. Florence [FI], and Herb. Leiden [L] (pres. 1849). Also dupl. (distrib. Kew) in Herb. Leningrad [LE] and in Herb. Oxford [OXF].

 

literature:

(1) cf. Curtis, Catal. Plants Penang, 1892, p. 100; Ridley in Journ. Str. Br. Roy. As. Soc. no 25, 1894, p. 166; Backer, Verkl. Woordenb., 1936, sub Norrisia.

 

biographical data:
