Bamler, Georg

 

(Source: Flora Malesiana ser. 1, 1: Cyclopaedia of collectors)

(Source: Flora Malesiana ser. 1, 1: Cyclopaedia of collectors, Addenda & Corrigenda)

 

Born: 1868, Burghaslach, Germany.

Died: Nov. 12, 1928, Siassi Rook Isl., Mand. Territory of New Guinea.

 

career:

A protestant missionary of the ‘Neuendettelsauer Missionsgesellschaft’ working in former German New Guinea, 1887-89; then transferred to the Tami Islands and in 1898 stationed again in former Kaiser-Wilhelmsland.1 Passed the last years of his life on Siassi Rook; his death was caused by a falling tree.

He is the author of some ethnographical papers.2

Amyema bamleri Dans. and several other plants were named after him.

 

Collecting localities:

He visited the Sias(s)i Islands with G. Schmiele in 1892 (?collected).-Tami Islands (1894, also a statement of Aug. 1898).-Former German New Guinea, NE. New Guinea: Sattelberg (Dec. 1898-99); 1908-14 from Logaueng etc., Wareo, Cromwell Mts, Sattelberg; autumn 1910 he climbed from Sialum via Mayawa summit to the source region of the Tewahe.

 

collections:

Herb. Berl. [B]: 121 nos from the Tami Islands (Algae, ferns and phanerogams) and 64 nos from the Sattelberg; 20 nos in the Show Museum. The plants from the Tami Islands were mainly collected during trips in 1894.3 Ferns in Herb. Bonaparte (= Paris [P]) (coll. 1913-14).4 Herb. Leiden [L]: Algae from the Tami Islands, phanerogams Sattelberg (coll. 1898-99), and ferns (coll. 1909). The labels bear a number and a letter, the letter indicating the locality, e.g. S for Sattelberg, etc.

Some papers refer to his collections.5
